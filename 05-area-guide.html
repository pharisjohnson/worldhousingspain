Altea is often called the cultural capital of the Costa Blanca, and for good reason. Its enchanting old town, crowned by the iconic blue and white dome of the Church of Our Lady of Consolation, has inspired artists and writers for generations. The narrow cobblestone streets, whitewashed houses adorned with colourful flowers, and stunning sea views create an atmosphere of timeless Mediterranean charm.

Beyond its artistic heritage, Altea offers an exceptional quality of life. The town boasts pristine sandy beaches, a vibrant restaurant scene featuring both traditional Spanish cuisine and international fine dining, and a thriving cultural calendar that includes the famous Festival of Moors and Christians. For discerning buyers, Altea presents a rare combination of authentic Spanish character and modern luxury living.

Market Overview

The Altea Property Market

Altea's property market caters to the luxury segment, with a focus on villas and fincas that offer privacy, space, and spectacular views. The town's strict building regulations have preserved its aesthetic character, ensuring that new developments complement the traditional Mediterranean architecture.

Property prices in Altea range from approximately €300,000 for apartments in established complexes to over €5 million for premium seafront villas. The most sought-after areas include Altea Hills, the old town, and the beachfront promenade.
