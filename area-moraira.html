Moraira is the Costa Blanca's best-kept secret — an intimate coastal town where luxury meets understated Mediterranean charm. Unlike its more famous neighbours, Moraira has retained a quiet exclusivity that discerning buyers treasure. Its sheltered coves with crystal-clear turquoise water, pine-clad hillsides dotted with elegant villas, and a friendly marina lined with seafood restaurants create an atmosphere of effortless sophistication.

The town's small scale is its greatest asset. Life here revolves around the sea: morning coffee at a beachfront café, afternoon sailing from the Club Náutico, sunset drinks overlooking the famous Peñón de Ifach in the distance. For those seeking a second home or permanent retreat that combines privacy, natural beauty, and world-class dining, Moraira is an unmatched choice on the Mediterranean coast.

Market Overview

The Moraira Property Market

Moraira commands a premium position in the Costa Blanca property market, driven by limited inventory and sustained international demand. The town's strict planning regulations have preserved its natural coastline and prevented overdevelopment, making well-located villas highly sought after.

The market is dominated by luxury villas with sea views, many set within private gated communities. Buyers are typically European — British, Dutch, German, and increasingly Scandinavian — seeking both permanent residences and high-yield holiday rentals. The most desirable areas include the hillsides of El Portet and the coastal enclaves overlooking the Mediterranean.
