Alicante is the beating heart of the Costa Blanca — a dynamic, cosmopolitan city where 3,000 years of history meet modern Mediterranean living. The iconic Castillo de Santa Bárbara crowns Mount Benacantil, watching over a city of palm-lined boulevards, Art Nouveau buildings, and one of Spain's most vibrant waterfront promenades.

The city offers an exceptional quality of life with world-class infrastructure, international schools, two universities, and a thriving business scene. Its gastronomy is legendary — from Michelin-starred restaurants to traditional tapas bars in the historic Santa Cruz quarter. The nightlife, cultural calendar, and annual Hogueras de San Juan festival rank among Spain's finest.

For property buyers, Alicante presents a compelling mix of urban apartments, beachfront residences, and suburban villas — all at more accessible prices than Barcelona or Madrid. The international airport connects to over 100 European destinations, making it a favourite for investors and second-home buyers alike.

Market Overview

The Alicante Property Market

Alicante's property market is one of the most dynamic on the Costa Blanca, driven by strong demand from both domestic buyers and international investors. The city offers a wide range of options, from historic apartments in the city centre to modern developments in growth districts like San Juan Playa and Albufereta.

The ongoing urban regeneration of the port area and the expansion of the tram network continue to drive appreciation. Rental yields remain strong thanks to year-round tourism and a growing student population, making Alicante a favourite for buy-to-let investors.
