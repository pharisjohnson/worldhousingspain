Sandy Beaches

Calpe is defined by its landmark — the Peñón de Ifach, a colossal limestone rock that rises 332 metres from the sea, connected to the shore by a narrow isthmus. This natural monument is the symbol of the Costa Blanca and the heart of Calpe's identity. A protected natural park, it offers hiking trails with breathtaking panoramic views and a haven for seabirds and native flora.

Beyond the Peñón, Calpe is a vibrant Mediterranean town with a dual personality. The old town clusters around the historic church and fishing port, where traditional red-sailed boats (llaüts) bob in the harbour and seafood restaurants serve the day's catch. The modern seafront stretches along the Levante and Arenal-Bol beaches, lined with promenades, chiringuitos, and luxury apartment blocks with front-line sea views.

Market Overview

The Calpe Property Market

Calpe's property market is defined by its iconic geography. Front-line apartments with direct views of the Peñón command the highest premiums, particularly along the Levante and Arenal-Bol beachfronts. The town offers a wider range of property types than most Costa Blanca destinations — from compact studio apartments to substantial detached villas in the hills above town.

Calpe attracts a diverse international buyer base, including Spanish nationals, northern Europeans, and increasingly remote workers drawn by the excellent infrastructure and year-round lifestyle. The rental market is strong, particularly during the summer season, making Calpe a popular choice for buy-to-let investors seeking reliable occupancy and yields.
